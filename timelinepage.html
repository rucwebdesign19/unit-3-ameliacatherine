1776

Only land-owning, white men can vote.

1856

The right to vote is granted to all white men.

1868

The 14th Amendment grants African Americans citizenship, but not the right to vote.

1870

The 15th Amendment prevents federal and/or state government from denying the right to vote based upon race.

1920

The 19th Amendment grants the right to vote to female citizens, however many women of color are not considered citizens.

1961

The 23rd Amendement allows DC residents to vote in presidential elections, but not congressional representation.

1965

The Voting Rights Act is passed, removing discriminatory barriers that kept many people of color from voting.

1971

In light of the Vietnam War, the voting age is lowered from 21 to 18 so that those old enough to serve the country were able to vote.

1993

The National Voter Registration Act is passed, making voting registration more accessible through the DMV and public assistance centers.

2020

Initiative to give man's best friend the right to vote!
